Rosa Luxemburg

The revolutionary woman

Picture of Rosa Luxemburg

Here's a time line of Rosa Luxemburg:

  • 1871 She was born in Zamosc, in the Russian Empire (now Poland).
  • 1873 Moved with her parents and siblings to Warsaw.
  • 1887 Finished her studies at Warsaw's Second Gymnasium for Girls.
  • 1889 Left Poland to study at Zurich University in Switzerland.
  • 1893 Co-founded the Social Democracy of the Kingdom of Poland(SDKP, later SDKPiL) with Leo Jogiches, Julian Marchlewski and Adolf Warszawski.
  • 1893 Attended the International Socialist Congress in Zurich.
  • 1896 Attended the International Socialist Congress in London.
  • 1897 Awarded her doctorate from Zurich Univeristy
  • 1898 Moved from Zurich to Berlin, Germany
  • 1900 Attended the ISC in Paris, France
  • 1904 Sentenced to two month imprisonment for "insulting the Kaiser".
  • 1904 Attended the ISC in Amsterdam, Netherlands.
  • 1905-1906 Participated in the '1905 Revolution' in Warsaw. Imprisoned for revolutionary activities from March-June 1906 in the Warsaw Citadel
  • 1906 Sentenced to two months imprisonment in Weimar, Germany
  • 1907 Attended Congress of the Russian Social Democrats in London, England.
  • 1913 Made a speech at Fechenheim, near Frankfurt, callin on German workers to refuse to take up arms against their French brothers. As a result, she was sentenced to one year's imprisonment.
  • 1914 Following the Reichtag vote of the previous day, in which the Social Democrats voted in favour of war credits, Luxemburg co-founded the anti-war Internationale Group' in Berlin, which later evolved into the Spartacus Leage.
  • 1915 Wrote the anti-war Junius Pamphlet.
  • 1916 Arrested in Berlin and held under 'protective custody' for the next two years.
  • 1917 It happened the 'February' and 'October Revolution' in Russia.
  • 1918 Released from Breslau Prison on the day that the Kaiser's abdication is announced and a new government is formed by Max von Baden, which seeks an armistice. Rosa returned to Berlin and began agitation with a new newspaper, 'Die Rote Fahre' ('The Reg Flag').
  • 1918 Fighting breaks out in Berlin between government forces and revolutionary sailors. The same year, co-founded the German Communist Party (KPD) with Karl Liebknecht, Leo Jogiches and others in Berlin.
  • 1919 Beginning of 'the Spartacist Rising' in Berlin, when armed workers demostrate against the removal of left-wing police chief Emil Eichhorn.
  • 1919 Rosa Luxemburg and Karl Liebknecht arrested by government ‘Freikorps’ and taken to the Eden Hotel, where they were interrogated. Liebknecht taken to Berlin’s Tiergarten and shot. Luxemburg rifle-butted and forced into a car, in which she was shot in the head. Her body is thrown into the Landwehr canal.
